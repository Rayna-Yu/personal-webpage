Code 4 Community
Code 4 Community (C4C) develops scalable technological solutions to help nonprofits in Boston maximize their impact. We are a team of passionate student developers partners with local organizations to create tools, apps, and systems that streamline operations, improve outreach, and drive meaningful change in the community.

This semester, I am working with Team BRANCH (Building Relationship Science for Advanced Networks in Communication and Health), a Boston-based nonprofit dedicated to research and develop innovative ways to strengthen the connection between clinicians and patients. By fostering trust, empathy, and compassion, BRANCH aims to improve healthcare outcomes. Guided by the concept of meliotropism, an orientation toward meaningful, prosocial, and health-promoting action, the team promotes interdisciplinary collaboration, ethical digital health tools, and international partnerships to help people thrive.

Nim Sum Player
Nim Sum Player is an interactive computer program that plays the classic mathematical strategy game Nim. The program leverages the binary properties of the XOR operation to calculate the Nim-sum of all heaps at each turn. By analyzing the bitwise representation of heap sizes, it determines an optimal move that transforms the current game state into a "zero Nim-sum" position, a provably safe state that guarantees eventual victory under perfect play. In essence, the program encodes the winning strategy discovered by C. Bouton in 1901.

Smart Image Shrinker
Smart Image Shrinker is a smarter way of shrinking an image. Instead of cropping and losing important information, or squishing which distorts the image, this program will calculate the "seam" with the least energy (least significant) seam in an image and deletes it. This program uses dynamic programing to optimize the run time in calculating the energy of each seam in the image.

Calendar Application
A comprehensive study of object-oriented design principles and software architecture through the development of a calendar application using the Model-View-Controller (MVC) architecture. The project emphasizes seperation of concern and modularity while showcasing best practices in encapsulation and polymorphism. Multiple design patterns were explored and implemented such as Builder, Controller, Decorator, and Observer to produce clean, well-documented, well-tested, and extensible code.
